# Rewardz Project Context

## Overview

Rewardz is being explored as a QR-based shared loyalty network for independent merchants.
The working idea is that customers could earn stamps or wallet credit across participating local businesses.
The current hypothesis is that a shared network might create merchant density and repeat customer behaviour at town level better than isolated single-shop loyalty schemes.

## Core Concept

- Customers might scan QR codes to collect stamps or wallet value.
- One possible mechanic is `10 stamps = reward or payout`.
- A candidate charging model is roughly `20p per scan` or about `1%` of transaction value.
- The platform might keep a smaller per-scan/platform cut.
- The concept is being framed as a shared network rather than just a merchant-specific punch card.

## Beachhead Market

- The current beachhead hypothesis is East Sussex.
- One proposed town sequence is Bexhill, then Hastings, then Eastbourne.
- Early focus may be independent cafés, with expansion to restaurants, barbers, and broader town-currency style use only if core retention is later proven.

## Near-Term Goal

- A near-term ambition is to test whether first-mover position could be established inside a six-month window.
- The working aim is to demonstrate merchant adoption, repeat scan behaviour, retention, and operational viability before any broader expansion.
- The board is trying to identify what evidence would suggest a competitor could or could not easily neutralise the concept.

## Unit Economics

- Current planning assumptions sometimes use `400 users`, `20 merchants`, and `1 town`.
- Indicative gross annual revenue has been discussed at about `£26k`.
- Indicative annual margin has been discussed at about `£6.5k`.
- These figures are explicitly provisional and should be challenged unless supported by evidence.

## Funding Position

- One funding narrative under discussion includes a `$1M` raise.
- One framing discussed is `1% equity`, implying a `$100M` valuation.
- That valuation should be treated as speculative and challengeable unless actual evidence is presented.
- Investor-facing discussion is likely to focus on ROI, downside protection, milestones, and exit path.

## Product And Technology

- One product direction is a BYOD merchant app for scanning and redemption.
- Another assumed component is a customer wallet app for loyalty state and rewards.
- QR security may need rotating or short-lived codes plus post-scan fraud controls.
- Payments assumptions currently reference Stripe Connect.
- Compliance assumptions currently lean toward considering FCA-readiness early rather than retrofitting later.

## Strategic Constraints

- The concept is currently intended to stay indie-first and community-oriented.
- Merchant density, scan frequency, trust, fraud resistance, and onboarding simplicity are being treated as likely core constraints.
- Expansion beyond the beachhead should be treated as contingent on explicit proof gates rather than intuition alone.
