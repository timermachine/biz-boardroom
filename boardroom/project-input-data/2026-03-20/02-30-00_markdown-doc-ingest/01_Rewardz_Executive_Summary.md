## The Opportunity

Rewardz is a proposed shared digital loyalty network designed exclusively for independent local merchants. The platform enables any independent business — coffee shops, restaurants, barbers, retailers — to offer a compelling, modern loyalty programme without proprietary hardware, technical expertise, or significant upfront cost.

Independent merchants currently have no credible answer to the loyalty infrastructure operated by major chains. Starbucks, Costa, and Tesco Clubcard invest tens of millions to build habit, data, and retention. The independent sector — representing a substantial portion of UK high street commerce — operates on paper stamp cards or no loyalty mechanism at all. Rewardz closes this gap.

## The Concept in Brief

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Platform Name** | Rewardz (working title; formerly explored as 'Tenthly') |
| **Core Mechanic** | Shared QR-based stamp/cash wallet loyalty network across participating independent merchants |
| **Revenue Model** | ~1% per transaction (approx. 3p–20p per scan depending on transaction value); platform retains ~5p margin per scan |
| **Merchant Cost** | Approximately 20p per scan or 1% of transaction value; zero hardware required (BYOD) |
| **Customer Reward** | Stamps accumulate across all network merchants; 10 stamps = free item or cash wallet payout |
| **Technology** | Cryptographic QR cycling (30-second refresh + post-scan invalidation), merchant app (BYOD), customer wallet app |
| **Compliance** | FCA Small Payment Institution registration; fund routing via Stripe Connect (platform does not hold customer funds) |
| **Beachhead Market** | East Sussex coastal towns: Bexhill (~8 venues), Hastings (~10 venues), Eastbourne (~9 venues) |
| **Stage** | Pre-product concept; no MVP built; research and go/no-go gate at Week 15 |
| **Target Date** | US and China market entry by Month 6 (subject to proof of concept and funding) |

## The Market Context

The UK coffee shop market alone is valued at £6.1–£6.7 billion (2025), with approximately 11,500 branded outlets and a significant independent segment. The US market is estimated at $54–$74 billion. Independent operators typically account for 30–40% of venues nationally but receive negligible technology and loyalty infrastructure investment compared to major chains.

Existing loyalty platforms predominantly serve chains or require costly hardware. Coalition networks such as Miconex have demonstrated that place-based shared loyalty is commercially viable, but their models rely on Business Improvement Districts for distribution and do not operate at the individual merchant transaction level that Rewardz proposes.

## Strategic Intent

- Establish first-mover position in the independent merchant shared loyalty category in the UK within 6 months.
- US and China market entry by Month 6, leveraging the platform's horizontal applicability beyond coffee.
- Pricing locked at approximately 1% of transaction value — designed to be structurally unbeatable for incumbents to match without cannibalising their own margins.
- Early adopter lock-in at current rates for 1–3 year terms, creating a compounding installed base advantage.
- Open architecture from day one: the platform is not a coffee loyalty app — it is a shared loyalty infrastructure layer applicable across any independent retail or hospitality vertical.
