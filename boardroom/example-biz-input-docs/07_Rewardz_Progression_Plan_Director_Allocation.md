# REWARDZ
## Recommended Progression Plan & Director Allocation

**CONFIDENTIAL | FOR C-LEVEL REVIEW ONLY | MARCH 2026**

---

## Introduction

This document sets out the recommended order of progression from initial concept through to funded, operating business — and maps the specialist roles and director-level appointments required at each stage. It is intended to guide C-level decision-making on sequencing, resource allocation, and leadership hiring priorities.

The sequencing follows a principle of **minimum viable action at each stage**: no resource is deployed prematurely, but no critical dependency is left unresolved. The platform cannot be built before the legal structure is clear; the platform cannot scale before unit economics are validated; international expansion cannot precede proof of domestic model. Each gate must be cleared before the next phase is authorised.

---

## Phase 0 — Foundation (Weeks 0–4)

This phase is about putting in place the minimum structures necessary to operate. No product is built. No external capital is deployed beyond personal founder resources.

| Action | Owner | Output | Gate Condition |
|---|---|---|---|
| Incorporate legal entity | Founder + solicitor | Company incorporated; shares issued; IP assigned to company. | Must complete before any investment conversation. |
| Commission FCA legal opinion | Founder + FCA counsel | Written opinion on SPI requirements, Stripe Connect structure, cash wallet classification. | Must complete before MVP design is finalised. |
| Engage technical co-founder or CTO | Founder | Identified individual with equity agreement; architecture responsibility allocated. | Must be in place before MVP scoping begins. |
| Initiate research programme | Founder + research lead | 7-workstream plan commissioned; merchant discovery interviews initiated. | Ongoing through Phase 1. |
| Identify China partner candidate | Founder | At least one China-based contact engaged in preliminary discussion. | Non-blocking but must begin now — lead time is long. |

---

## Phase 1 — Validation (Weeks 4–15)

The primary objective of Phase 1 is **proof, not product**. Merchant discovery interviews, consumer research, and technical feasibility work are conducted. The Phase 1 output is the Week 15 Go/No-Go decision — the first formal C-level gate.

### Key Activities

- Complete all 7 research workstreams (market sizing, consumer behaviour, merchant demand, unit economics, tech feasibility, regulatory, GTM).
- Conduct minimum 20 merchant discovery interviews across East Sussex beachhead territory.
- Commission MVP technical scoping — architecture, build cost, timeline, team requirements.
- Receive and review FCA legal opinion; determine registration pathway.
- Produce Week 15 Go/No-Go scorecard for C-level review.

### Week 15 Go/No-Go Scorecard

| Criterion | Go Threshold | No-Go Signal |
|---|---|---|
| **Merchant demand** | Minimum 15 of 20 interviewed merchants express strong interest at 1% pricing. | Majority price-resistant or unwilling to use BYOD. |
| **Consumer appetite** | Survey data supports cross-merchant loyalty app adoption. | Strong preference for physical cards; low app adoption intent. |
| **Unit economics** | Modelled economics validated within 20% by real merchant transaction data. | Material divergence from model renders economics unviable. |
| **Technical feasibility** | MVP can be scoped at under £80,000 and 6-month build timeline. | Technical complexity or cost materially exceeds estimates. |
| **Regulatory pathway** | FCA counsel confirms viable registration pathway with Stripe Connect structure. | FCA requires fund-holding structure that creates unacceptable liability. |

## Phase 2 — Build (Months 4–9)

Conditional on a positive Week 15 Go/No-Go outcome, the MVP build phase commences. Capital deployment (from pre-seed round or bridging finance) begins at this stage.

| Action | Owner | Timeline | Dependency |
|---|---|---|---|
| Close pre-seed funding | CEO + CFO | Month 4–5 | Go/No-Go positive; investor term sheet agreed. |
| Commence MVP development | CTO + dev team | Month 4–9 | Pre-seed funding received; FCA legal structure confirmed. |
| Sign first 20 merchant agreements | CCO / Merchant Director | Month 5–7 | Merchant agreement template approved by legal. |
| FCA SPI application submission | CFO + FCA counsel | Month 5 | Legal opinion received; Stripe Connect structure confirmed. |
| Initiate US scoping (entity, banking, legal) | CEO + US counsel | Month 5–6 | Non-blocking on UK launch but must not be deferred further. |
| Activate China operating partner | CEO + China contact | Month 5–6 | Partner identified in Phase 0. |
| Platform security penetration test | CTO + external security firm | Month 8 | Pre-launch requirement; cannot be deferred post-launch. |

## Director Roles & Appointment Sequence

| Role | Primary Accountability | Appointment Phase | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Chief Executive Officer (CEO)** | Overall strategy, investor relations, culture, final accountability on all decisions. | Day 0 — Founder | Sets direction; holds the vision; Art of War thinking; drives fundraising narrative. |
| **Chief Technology Officer (CTO)** | Platform architecture, MVP build, security, infrastructure, technical team. | **Phase 0 — critical early hire** | Most time-sensitive hire. The CTO shapes architecture decisions that cannot easily be undone. Equity stake essential. |
| **Chief Financial Officer (CFO)** | Financial model, investor reporting, FCA compliance, treasury, unit economics. | Phase 0–1 (fractional initially) | Can be fractional in Phase 0–1. Must be full-time before pre-seed close. Owns the overfund buffer and tranche mechanics. |
| **Chief Commercial Officer (CCO)** | Merchant acquisition, partnerships, BID relationships, early adopter programme, market activation. | Phase 1–2 | The field general for beachhead merchant acquisition. Direct-to-merchant experience essential. |
| **Chief Product Officer (CPO)** | Product roadmap, customer app UX, merchant app UX, feature prioritisation, customer research. | Phase 2 (post-funding) | Initially the CTO may double as CPO. Dedicated CPO needed when platform is live and feedback volume justifies it. |
| **General Counsel / Legal Director** | Merchant agreements, investor agreements, FCA registration, GDPR, IP protection. | Phase 0 (external counsel) | Retained external counsel initially. In-house appointment justified at Series A. |
| **Director of Merchant Success** | Post-onboarding merchant relationship, retention, upsell, early adopter programme management. | Phase 3 (post-launch) | Retains rather than acquires. Critical for preventing churn after lock-in period expires. |
| **Director of International Expansion** | US market entry, China partnership management, regulatory compliance per territory. | Phase 2 | China entry requires local expertise — a China-based operator or JV partner, not a UK director with China interest. |
| **Chief Marketing Officer (CMO)** | Consumer brand, app marketing, merchant marketing, PR, community impact communications. | Phase 2–3 | Early-stage marketing is founder-led and scrappy. Formal CMO justified post-Series A. |

## Recommended C-Level Decision Sequence

| Decision | Recommended Timing | Decision Owner | Input Required |
|---|---|---|---|
| Proceed to research programme | Day 0 | CEO | Concept brief reviewed; initial funding confirmed. |
| CTO / technical co-founder appointment | Week 1–2 | CEO | Candidate identified; equity terms agreed. |
| FCA legal counsel engagement | Week 1–2 | CEO + CFO | Counsel shortlist; scope of engagement agreed. |
| **Go / No-Go at Week 15** | Week 15 | **Full C-level** | Complete research programme scorecard. |
| MVP scope and build cost approval | Week 16 | CEO + CTO + CFO | Technical scoping document; build cost estimate. |
| Pre-seed investor term sheet approval | Month 4–5 | CEO + CFO + legal | Term sheet with all four protection mechanisms. |
| Merchant agreement approval | Month 5 | CEO + CCO + legal | Template reviewed by legal; pilot terms confirmed. |
| **Platform launch authorisation** | Month 9 | **Full C-level** | Security pen test complete; FCA status confirmed; 20 merchants signed; app QA passed. |
| International entry authorisation | Month 6 (assessment) | CEO + CFO | Minimum viable international presence defined; cost and legal requirements assessed. |
| Seed round initiation | Month 12–15 | CEO + CFO | Post-launch traction data; metrics validate seed narrative. |

---

> **The single most important near-term decision is the CTO appointment.**
>
> All other Phase 0 actions can be managed by the founding team. The CTO appointment cannot be deferred — it is the most consequential hire the business will make, because every subsequent technical decision will be built on or constrained by the architecture choices made in the first 90 days.
