## 1. Valuation Anchor

The founders propose an implied **post-money valuation of $100M** from the initial funding round. This is an aggressive pre-product position, explicitly acknowledged as such.

The justification for this position rests on four pillars:

- **Total addressable market:** the independent merchant loyalty gap is a global problem affecting millions of businesses. Even a single-digit percentage of the UK independent hospitality market generates a substantial platform revenue base.
- **First-mover structural advantage:** the 6-month window to establish an installed base of early-adopter merchants creates a network effect moat that is time-sensitive.
- **Pricing model defensibility:** the 1% fee structure is designed to be structurally untenable for incumbents to match — creating a durable price position.
- **International applicability:** the platform architecture is designed for horizontal deployment across verticals and geographies. The UK coffee beachhead is the proof vehicle, not the market ceiling.

## 3. The Four Investor Protection Mechanisms

### 3.1 Non-Renegotiation Clause

Any signed Letter of Intent (LOI) fixes valuation and material terms. Post-LOI attempts to re-trade trigger a contractual penalty — proposed as forfeiture of deal exclusivity or a 0.5% equity claw-back. This protects the founders from investor sandbagging after due diligence.

### 3.2 Overfund Buffer

Each round targets **1.5× the stated operational requirement**. If the business requires $1M to reach the next milestone, the target raise is $1.5M. The excess 0.5× is held in a milestone reserve account, accessible only upon defined KPI achievement. This provides runway protection against cost overruns, market delays, and harder-than-expected subsequent raises.

The cardinal rule: **never arrive at a fundraise on fumes.** Subsequent round conversations begin when the business has 6+ months of runway, not 6 weeks.

### 3.3 Milestone-Locked Tranches

| Tranche | % of Round | Release Condition |
|---|---|---|
| **Tranche 1** | 50% | Released at signing. |
| **Tranche 2** | 30% | Released upon Milestone A (e.g., MVP live; minimum 20 merchant agreements signed; minimum 400 active app users). |
| **Tranche 3** | 20% | Released upon Milestone B (e.g., first £X revenue; US or China operating agreement signed). |

Milestone definitions are negotiated at signing and documented in the investment agreement. Ambiguous milestone definitions are not acceptable — each must be binary and independently verifiable.

### 3.4 Anti-Dilution Provision

The founders propose offering early investors **broad-based weighted average anti-dilution** protection. This is the market-standard mechanism: it adjusts the investor's conversion price in the event of a down round, calculated on the weighted average of all shares outstanding. Full ratchet anti-dilution — which would be heavily investor-favourable — is not offered.

This mechanism is designed to make investors comfortable with the $100M pre-product valuation anchor by providing downside protection, without creating a mechanism that would catastrophically dilute founders in an adverse scenario.
